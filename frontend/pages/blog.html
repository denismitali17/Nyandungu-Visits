Discover the Heart of Nyandungu Eco-Park: A Journey into Rwanda’s Green Haven

Nestled in the bustling city of Kigali, Nyandungu Eco-Park is a serene escape and a triumph of conservation efforts, blending Rwanda’s natural beauty with eco-friendly tourism. As the first restored wetland park in the country, Nyandungu stands as a testament to Rwanda’s dedication to protecting its environment and fostering sustainable tourism.

What to Expect at Nyandungu Eco-Park

Nyandungu Eco-Park offers visitors a peaceful experience immersed in nature. With walking trails winding through lush greenery, diverse wetlands, and observation points, there’s something for every nature lover. Guided tours provide unique insights into the park’s rich ecosystem, showcasing local plants, bird species, and more.

For those wanting to extend their stay, Nyandungu’s restaurant offers a delightful Saturday brunch from 9 AM to 2 PM—a perfect way to enjoy a delicious meal surrounded by the beauty of nature. Or, if you’re looking to start your journey early, book a tour available every day from 9 AM to 6 PM for just 1,000 RWF per person.

A Community Effort Toward Conservation

Nyandungu Eco-Park represents more than just a place to visit; it’s a model of community-centered conservation. By reinvesting tourism revenue into local projects, Nyandungu supports job creation, environmental education, and sustainable practices that benefit the surrounding community. Visitors have the chance to directly contribute to these efforts by participating in community events or donating to ongoing conservation projects.
